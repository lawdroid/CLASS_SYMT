YM anchor — where does β come from?

The Martin–Koh afterglow paper (April 2026) does not pick a value of β. It bounds β by structural conditions on the kernel Ψ(r) and the late-branch stability. This page walks through those equations interactively. ← back to summary

The β number line — three regions, Eqs. (94) and (96)

The crossing condition β · Ψ_max > 1 turns into a lower bound on β:

β > 1 / Ψ_max = 3√3 / 2 ≈ 2.598

The stability upper bound comes from requiring the late-branch decaying solution r → 0 to remain stable. With the (1 + 4β)/3 guard on c_D, the full structurally-allowed crossing window is:

3√3 / 2 < β < 2√3 (≈ 2.598 to ≈ 3.464)

So the β line is partitioned into four named regions:

3a
β = 0 exactly. Isolated YM tail, no DE↔matter exchange. The Σ̇ relaxation falls back to the source-free form (Eq. 25 in the YM paper).
3b
β ∈ (0, 2.4]. Coupled but below the crossing threshold — w_eff stays ≥ −1 for all r.
3c
β ∈ [2.598, 3.464]. Inside the allowed crossing window — w_eff dips below −1 near r ≈ 3.73. B1-narrow restricts to [2.65, 2.75] to skirt the BP-regulator δ-sensitivity (see Phase 3 preregistration §6).
forbidden
β > 2√3. Late-branch stability guard fails; (1 − β Ψ) flips sign on the trajectory in a way the regulator cannot cleanly absorb.

What this means for the MCMC result

Theory carves the β line into a discrete menu: β = 0 (3a), β ∈ (0, 2.4] (3b), β ∈ [2.65, 2.75] (3c). Pre-registering one chain per region was the way to fairly test all three. The result was:

  • The data does not pick a region. All three branches are consistent with current data.
  • The data does not pick a β within any region. Each branch's β marginal mirrors its prior (see explorer).
  • The structural bounds are real. 2√3 / 9 is the kernel ceiling no matter what data ever says, and 3√3/2 is the firm crossing threshold below which phantom mimicry is impossible.

So "YM selects β" in the sense of bounding it to discrete structural windows, not in the sense of picking a numerical value. A microscopic derivation tying β to the YM coupling g_D, the gluon condensate ⟨G²⟩, or ε_Σ — none of which is in the published papers — would be the route to picking a value from theory.
