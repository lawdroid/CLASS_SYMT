Headline (three things, in plain language)

  1. β on its own is unpinned; the physical coupling g is bounded. The 3b marginal is flat across the full prior (0, 2.4], and the 3c marginal mirrors its narrow B1 prior, so the data has no opinion on β by itself. β enters the dynamics only through g ≡ β/c_D, and that combination is bounded: g < 0.20 at 95% (see the next card).
  2. c_D is constrained from above. Branch medians sit at c_D ≈ 22.7 / 23.7 / 28.7 (3a/3b/3c) with the 95% region capped near c_D ≲ 85–90 (per-branch q95: 83.5 / 85.2 / 88.1). This is the main data-driven bound.
  3. H₀, Ωₘ, σ₈, and w_X are robust across branches. w_X is tight at –0.99, effectively a cosmological constant, in all three branches; it equals the intrinsic value -1 + 1/(3c_D) at the fitted c_D. H₀, Ωₘ, σ₈ shift by less than ~1σ between branches.

No bimodality anywhere. Every 2D KDE is a single ridge. See the geometry-lesson page for why this matters and what the §3.11 indirect-signal trap looks like.

The coupling

β enters the equations of motion only through the ratio g ≡ β/c_D, so β on its own is unidentified while the physically meaningful combination is bounded. A flat marginal in the β basis and a bounded marginal in the g basis are the same statement (Savage–Dickey). Post-processing the 3b chain sample-by-sample in the g basis gives:

Defensible Claims

  • The framework is consistent with current data across all three pre-registered branches.
  • c_D < ~85–90 at 95% across all three branches (q95 = 83.5 / 85.2 / 88.1).
  • w_X ≈ –0.99 across all three branches, effectively a cosmological constant in the data.
  • The physical coupling is bounded: g ≡ β/c_D < 0.20 at 95% (median g = 0.038), set through the geometry and CMB channels.
  • β on its own is not discriminated within any of the explored priors, which is the same statement as the g bound in a different basis.

All three |Δ log Z| < 1: the data neither prefer nor exclude the afterglow model, with a mild positive lean toward finite c_D consistent with DESI DR2's soft dynamical-dark-energy hint. The quoted uncertainty is dominated by a boundary-extrapolation systematic of the density-ratio method; a definitive PolyChord nested-sampling computation is staged. The one microscopic amplitude the chains inform is the initial pressure deficit ε_Σ ≈ 0.01, a plausible hidden-sector value.
