The 4 Quantities, Simply

Four things. That's it. Slide and see how they connect.

1. ρ_X — dark energy density

How much dark energy is packed into a cubic meter of space. More ρ_X = more pushing out.

ρ_X = 0.70   (in units of today's critical density)

2. p_X — dark energy pressure

Dark energy has negative pressure. That's what drives the universe apart.

p_X = w · ρ_X = -0.67

Negative pressure sounds weird. Think of it as a stretched rubber sheet pulling outward on whatever holds it.

3. w — the ratio that ties them together

The equation of state. One number. It says "pressure is this many times density."

w = p_X / ρ_X
w = −1 + 1/(3·c_D) = -0.67

• If w = −1 exactly → cosmological constant (pure Λ).
• If w > −1 → "quintessence-like", dilutes slowly.
• If w < −1 → "phantom" (the afterglow model forbids this at classical level).

4. a — the scale factor (cosmic clock)

One number that tells you how big the universe is now compared to today.

a = 1
today
a = 0.5
universe half as big → redshift z = 1
a = 0.1
tenth as big → z = 9
a → 0
Big Bang

Redshift ↔ scale factor:

1 + z = 1/a

"Time in H scale" = log(a) = N (e-folds). CLASS steps the universe in log(a) because it spans ~30 decades uniformly from Big Bang to now. One e-fold = universe doubled (well, × e ≈ 2.72).

Now watch ρ_X evolve with a

In the simple β=0 limit of the afterglow model:

ρ_X(a) = ρ_X(today) · a−1/c_D

For c_D = 1 → ρ_X dilutes like a-1 (slower than matter's a-3, faster than Λ's a0).
For c_D → ∞ → ρ_X becomes constant (Λ limit).

The whole story in one picture
